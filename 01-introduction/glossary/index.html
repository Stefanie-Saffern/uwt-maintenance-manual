    1.3.2 Definitions

    Technical Terms

    Carrier frequency The frequency of a carrier wave, measured in cycles per second, or Hertz, that is modulated to transmit signals.

    Demodulation The process by which modulated electrical/digital signals are converted back into the original unmodulated data/information.

    Hydrophone An underwater microphone that detects sound waves.

    LRU (Line Replaceable Unit) A modular component designed to be replaced without specialized tools.

    Ping An acoustic pulse.

    Transducer Active sonar transducer that emits an acoustic signal or pulse of sound into the water.

    Hardware Controls

    Rotary Control Knob * Adjusts any parameter value that uses + /– icons. * The knob is normally latched; rotating it unlatches it. * Pressing the knob closes the current menu level and returns to the previous menu while saving the selected value. * If not pressed, the knob re-latches automatically after 3 seconds.

    Single Sideband (SSB) * A form of amplitude modulation (AM) that transmits only one sideband (upper or lower). * Cuts bandwidth in half and saves power compared to conventional AM. * Provides better signal efficiency and reduced noise.
